Ethics Principles Reference Table

22 Mar, 2025

This table outlines fundamental ethical principles applicable across various domains, including healthcare, law, and business. Each principle is defined, illustrated with a practical example, and justified by its importance in fostering ethical conduct. The principles encompass respect for autonomy, non-maleficence, beneficence, justice, veracity, fidelity, and self-respect, collectively forming a framework for moral decision-making and interpersonal interactions. This concise overview serves as a foundational guide to understanding and applying these core ethical values in professional and personal contexts.

ENG

PrincipleDefinitionExampleWhy it’s important
Respect for AutonomyRespect individuals’ freedoms and decision-making abilitiesA patient has the right to refuse medical treatment.Ensures individuals’ decisions are honored and respected.
Non-MaleficenceAvoid causing harm to othersA doctor should not prescribe medication that could cause harm.Prevents harm to others, ensuring actions are not detrimental to well-being or safety.
BeneficenceAct in ways that benefit othersA nurse provides emotional support to a patient.Promotes others’ health, happiness, and well-being.
JusticeAct fairly and impartiallyA judge applies the law equally to all parties.Ensures fairness, equity, and impartiality.
VeracityAct with honesty and truthfulnessA company does not misrepresent its products.Promotes trust and credibility.
FidelityFulfill commitments and obligationsAn employee honors their contract.Demonstrates respect for commitments and reinforces bonds between individuals and organizations.
Self-RespectValue and respect oneself and one’s moral valuesA person prioritizes self-care.Promotes a positive sense of identity, confidence, and personal growth.
